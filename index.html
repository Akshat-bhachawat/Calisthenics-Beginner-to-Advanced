Zero to Superhuman
Progression & Deload Rules
  • Phases are milestones, not deadlines — advance only when the exit checklist for your current phase is cleared, never just because a week count passed.
  • Increase reps before increasing difficulty. Only move a movement to its next progression once every set hits the top of its rep range, with clean form, for two consecutive sessions.
  • If form breaks down on any set, regress one step rather than pushing through with bad reps.
  • Every 4th week of a phase is a deload week: cut volume to roughly 60%, skip max-effort attempts and HIIT, keep everything else at normal intensity.
  • Don't advance phases while carrying persistent joint pain, poor sleep, or lingering soreness from the week before — resolve that first, even if the checklist is otherwise done.
